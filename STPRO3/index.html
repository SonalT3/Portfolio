Project Overview

Brief Introduction

Objective: To build an all-in-one fitness platform with workout plans, meal tracking, and live training sessions.

Target Audience: Fitness enthusiasts, beginners, and personal trainers.

Features: Homepage, Workout Library, Trainer Profiles, Meal Tracker, Live Classes, and Community Forum.

Problem Statement

Research, surveys, and feedbacks to reavel Workout knowledge and expertise.

* Many fitness enthusiasts struggle to find a centralized platform that combines workout tracking, meal planning, live classes, and community engagement.

* Existing solutions are often fragmented, leading to a disjointed experience.

* The challenge is to create a comprehensive website where users can track their workouts, meals, and progress, access live trainer-led classes, and interact with a motivating community, all in one place.

Research & Insights

User Research and Pain Points

Competitor Research: Examined MyFitnessPal, Nike Training Club, and Fitbit to understand fitness app trends.

User Interviews: Conducted informal surveys with gym-goers to identify key motivators and pain points.

Key Findings: Users want easy tracking, personalized recommendations, and a supportive community.

  • Difficulty Tracking
  • Limited Personalization
  • Low Engagement
  • Complex Navigation
  • Time Constraints
  • Unclear Progress

Competitor Analysis

1. MyFitnessPal

• ✅ Extensive food database with barcode scanning. • ✅ Tracks calories and macros in detail. • ❌ Free version has limited features (premium needed for deeper insights).

2. Nike Training Club

• ✅ Professional, trainer-led workouts for all levels. • ✅ Free access to high-quality training programs. • ❌ No meal tracking feature, only workout-based.

3. Fitbit App

• ✅ Tracks steps, workouts, heart rate, and meals. • ✅ Syncs well with Fitbit wearables. • ❌ Requires Fitbit device for full experience.

4. Strava

• ✅ Strong social community and leaderboard features. • ✅ Great for tracking running and cycling activities. • ❌ No meal tracking or general workout library.
